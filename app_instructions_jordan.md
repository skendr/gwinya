A gamified self-management app that helps people with dysphagia understand their condition, practise safe routines, remember personalised strategies, track symptoms, build confidence, and know when to seek help.

The app should be designed for people who may experience:

* fear or anxiety around eating and drinking  
* fatigue during meals  
* coughing, choking or wet voice episodes  
* uncertainty about safe foods and fluids  
* reduced confidence eating socially  
* memory or cognitive difficulties  
* speech, language or communication difficulties  
* reduced hand function or visual access needs  
* changing symptoms over time

| Function | What it does |
| ----- | ----- |
| Learn | Short dysphagia lessons in plain language |
| Prepare | Pre-meal readiness checks |
| Prompt | Reminders for personalised strategies |
| Track | Symptoms, confidence, food/fluid tolerance |
| Practise | Safe-swallow routines or prescribed exercises |
| Reflect | Notice patterns over time |
| Escalate | Know when to seek help |
| Participate | Support eating out, social meals and communication with others |

**Before a meal**

The user opens the app and sees:

* Am I alert enough to eat/drink?  
* Am I sitting upright?  
* Do I have the right food/fluid texture?  
* What are my strategies today?  
* Do I feel worried or breathless before starting?

**During a meal**

The app may provide optional prompts:

* take your time  
* small mouthfuls or sips  
* use your prescribed strategy  
* pause if coughing  
* stop if breathing feels difficult

This needs to be customisable, because some people will find live prompts helpful and others may find them intrusive.

**After a meal**

The user logs:

* coughing/choking episodes  
* wet voice  
* fatigue  
* food/fluid avoided  
* confidence level  
* any new concerns  
* whether they used their strategies

Over time, the app shows patterns: “You reported more coughing with evening meals” or “You feel more confident when meals are slower.”

The app should allow an SLT or trained clinician to enter:

* recommended food texture level  
* recommended fluid level  
* prescribed strategies  
* exercises, if appropriate  
* foods to avoid  
* supervision needs, if any  
* red flags for that individual  
* review date  
* emergency instructions

The app **should not**:

* diagnose dysphagia  
* decide someone’s diet level on its own  
* recommend thickened fluids independently  
* prescribe exercises without clinical input  
* tell someone to keep eating if they are choking, breathless or distressed  
* claim to detect aspiration reliably  
* replace an SLT, doctor or emergency care

The app **can**:

* support a plan already made with a clinician  
* help users remember and practise strategies  
* help users track symptoms  
* flag changes that need review  
* explain dysphagia in accessible language  
* support safer decision-making

**Data the app may collect**

For the user profile:

* diagnosis or cause of dysphagia, if known  
* current diet/fluid recommendations  
* prescribed strategies  
* communication needs  
* vision/hearing/access needs  
* language preference  
* cognitive or memory support needs  
* anxiety/confidence around meals  
* goals, such as eating out, drinking more safely, or managing fatigue

For daily use:

* meals completed  
* symptoms  
* strategy use  
* confidence  
* fatigue  
* food/fluid difficulties  
* hydration concerns  
* avoided foods  
* social participation barriers  
* questions for the SLT

For learning:

* lessons completed  
* quiz responses  
* repeated misunderstandings  
* reminders used  
* preferred prompt style  
* engagement pattern

**Possible app modules**

A patient-facing version could have modules like:

| Module | Purpose |
| ----- | ----- |
| Understanding my swallow | Explains dysphagia in plain language |
| My recommendations | Shows the person’s personalised plan |
| Before I eat | Readiness and safety checklist |
| My strategies | Reminds them of prescribed strategies |
| Food and drink confidence | Helps identify suitable textures |
| Eating out and social meals | Supports participation beyond the home |
| What changed today? | Symptom and confidence tracking |
| When to get help | Red flags and escalation |
| My progress | Shows learning, confidence and routines |
| Questions for my SLT | Builds a list for review appointments |
